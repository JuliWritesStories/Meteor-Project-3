Welcome to Meteorite Landing Page!!

Origin:

Meteorites are fragments of asteroids or other bodies in our solar system that have survived the journey through Earth's atmosphere and landed on the surface.

Composition:

Meteorites can be composed of various materials, including rock, metal, and even organic compounds. Some meteorites contain rare elements not typically found on Earth.

Impact craters:

When a meteorite strikes the Earth's surface, it can create impact craters of varying sizes, depending on the size and speed of the meteorite.

Classification:

Meteorites are classified into three main types: stony meteorites, iron meteorites, and stony-iron meteorites. Each type has distinct characteristics based on its composition.

Size arrange

Meteorites can vary in size from tiny grains to large masses weighing several tons. The largest meteorites found on Earth are known as "iron meteorites."

scientific Importance:

Studying meteorites provides valuable insights into the formation and evolution of our solar system. They can also contain clues about the early history of planets, including Earth.
